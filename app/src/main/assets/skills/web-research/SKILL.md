name: web-research
description: Research current, niche, or disputed questions on the web and produce a source-backed answer. Use when facts may have changed, primary documentation is required, recommendations carry meaningful cost, or the user asks for deep research.
license: Apache-2.0
compatibility: Requires web search and page-reading tools.
---

Turn the request into a small set of answerable questions. Note the current date, the required jurisdiction or platform, and what evidence would change the conclusion before searching.

Research efficiently:

1. Search for primary sources first: official documentation, standards, policy text, source repositories, filings, datasets, or original research.
2. Use secondary sources to discover context or disagreements, not to replace available primary evidence.
3. Open the supporting page, verify its publication or update date, and confirm that it states the claimed fact in the relevant version or jurisdiction.
4. For consequential or disputed claims, corroborate with an independent authoritative source and explain any conflict.
5. Prefer direct, stable pages over search snippets. Quote sparingly and preserve the meaning of the source.
6. Stop when the evidence answers the question; more tabs are not more certainty.

Separate sourced facts, calculations, and inference. State uncertainty when sources are incomplete or stale. Never invent a citation, imply that a source supports a broader claim than it does, or hide a material limitation behind confident wording.

Lead with the answer. Place descriptive links beside the claims they support, include exact dates or versions when relevant, and end with the practical implication or decision.
